1) (4 points) Radius vector of each point of solid body moves along the curve $R(t)=S(t)R(0)$, where

$S(t)=\left( \begin{array}{lll}\cos\varphi& -\sin\varphi& 0\\\cos\theta\sin\varphi&\cos\theta\cos\varphi&-\sin\theta\\\sin\theta\sin\varphi&\sin\theta\cos\varphi&\cos\theta\end{array}\right ),\mbox{ where }\varphi=\varphi(t),\theta=\theta(t)$

$R(t)=\left(\begin{array}{l}x(t)\\y(t)\\z(t)\end{array}\right)$

  a) Prove that linear map S(t) corresponds to the motion of solid body, i.e. S is orthogonal matrix.

  b) We know that velocity of each point $R(t)$ is equal to $v(t)=\dot{R}(t)=\dot{S}R(0)=\dot{S}S^{-1}R(t)=\dot{S}S^{T}R(t)=\Omega R(t)$.

Find $\Omega=\dot{S}S^T.$

  c) Find vector of angular velocity $\omega(t)$ that corresponds to $\Omega(t)$, i.e. $\Omega R=w\wedge R.$

  d) For each time $t$ find the instantaneous axis of rotation (the axis along which $v(t)=0$)
