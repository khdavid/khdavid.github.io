1) $\overrightarrow{S}(t)=(R_0\sin\omega t, R_0\cos\omega t,v_0t)$, where $R_0,v_0$ - constants.

 a) $\overrightarrow{v}(t)-?$(velocity)

 b) $\overrightarrow{a}(t)-?$(acceleration)

 c) $\overrightarrow{a}(t)\cdot\overrightarrow{v}(t)-?$

2) Prove that:

$(\overrightarrow{a},\overrightarrow{b},\overrightarrow{c})'=(\overrightarrow{a}',\overrightarrow{b},\overrightarrow{c})+(\overrightarrow{a},\overrightarrow{b}',\overrightarrow{c})+(\overrightarrow{a},\overrightarrow{b},\overrightarrow{c}')$

where $(\overrightarrow{a},\overrightarrow{b},\overrightarrow{c})=(\overrightarrow{a}\wedge\overrightarrow{b})\cdot\overrightarrow{c}$.

In the proof you may use Leibniz rule for scalar and vector products.
